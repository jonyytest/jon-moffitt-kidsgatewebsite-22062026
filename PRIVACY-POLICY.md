## The short version

The Kids Gate is a learning platform for children aged 5–12. Here is the plain-language summary; the full detail follows below.

- A **parent or guardian** creates and controls the account. We do not knowingly let children sign themselves up.
- From a child we collect only what we need to run their lessons — a **first name or nickname, age or grade level, and their learning activity** (answers, progress, achievements).
- We use a child's answers to **automatically choose what they should learn next**. This is an automated personalisation system. We do **not** use children's data to train artificial-intelligence models, and we do **not** use it for advertising.
- The platform is **ad-free**. We do **not** sell or share your or your child's personal information, and we do **not** build marketing or advertising profiles.
- **You are in control.** As a parent you can view your child's information, share it with their teacher if you want, add their achievement level to a local or national comparison service, and check and correct their lessons and quiz answers — and only a parent can ask us to delete it, at any time, by emailing **support@thekidsgate.com**.

## 4. How we use personal information

We use personal information to:

- **Provide the Services** — create and manage accounts and child profiles, deliver lessons, save progress, and operate the parent dashboard.
- **Personalise learning** — use a child's answers, accuracy and progress to automatically select suitable lessons and difficulty (see Section 4.1).
- **Operate rewards** — award tokens, achievements and leaderboard placements to encourage consistent learning (see Section 4.2).
- **Communicate with you** — send service and account messages (sign-up confirmation, billing and renewal notices, security alerts, responses to support requests), educational progress updates about your child, and marketing and promotional emails. Marketing is sent **only to parents and guardians — never to children** — and every marketing email includes an unsubscribe link (see Section 13).
- **Keep the Services safe** — detect, prevent and investigate fraud, abuse, and security issues, and enforce our terms.
- **Provide comparative insights** — show parents aggregated, anonymised comparisons (for example, how a child's earned tokens compare with the average for other learners) so progress has context. Other children's data used in these comparisons is aggregated and does not identify any individual child.
- **Improve the Services** — analyse aggregated and de-identified usage to improve content quality and performance.
- **Comply with the law** — meet our legal, tax, accounting and regulatory obligations.

### 4.1 Automated personalisation (how the "adaptive" learning works)

The Kids Gate uses an **automated system** that reads a child's responses — such as which answers are correct, how accurate they are, and what they have completed — and uses that to **decide which lesson or activity the child should do next** and at what difficulty.

Important points:

- This is **automated decision-making for the sole purpose of personalising education.** It does not produce legal effects or similarly significant effects on the child.
- We do **not** use children's personal information to **train artificial-intelligence models.**
- We do **not** use this information for advertising, marketing, or to build profiles about you or your child for any purpose other than delivering and tracking learning.
- A parent can review the results of this personalisation in the parent dashboard and can contact us with any questions.

### 4.2 Leaderboards and public display

To make learning motivating, the Services include leaderboards and prize draws.

**Leaderboards.** A child only appears in leaderboard groupings **when a parent has given consent**. Where a parent has consented, a child may be shown to other users by their **first name or nickname, country, avatar and/or score**, and rankings may be grouped by country, state or city, grade and subject. To protect children, we recommend the use of a **nickname rather than a real name**, and we apply safe-display rules.

**Prize draws.** Active learners may be entered into monthly prize draws. Prizes are **digital (such as gift-card codes)** and are delivered to the **parent's account email address** — we do not collect any additional personal information (such as a shipping address) to fulfil prizes. If we announce winners, we identify them only by nickname/display name and country; we do not publish any other information about a child. If a future prize requires physical delivery, we will collect the parent's delivery details at that time with the parent's consent.

### 4.3 Legal bases (for users in Indonesia and Thailand)

Where the law requires a legal basis to process personal information, we rely on:

- **Performance of a contract** — to provide the Services you sign up for.
- **Consent** — for a child's participation (given by a parent or guardian), and for any optional features. Consent can be withdrawn at any time.
- **Legitimate interests** — to keep the Services secure, prevent fraud, and improve our product, balanced against your rights.
- **Legal obligation** — to comply with laws that apply to us.

## 5. Children's privacy and parental consent

Protecting children is central to how we operate. The Services are designed for children aged 5–12. There is no strict minimum age — a parent or guardian decides whether the content suits their child — and the Services are intended to be set up and supervised by a **parent or legal guardian.**

**How consent works.** Browsing our website does not require an account. A child cannot create an account — accounts are created only by adults: a parent or guardian, or a teacher or school as described in Section 14. A parent or guardian creates an account by signing up, confirming they are 18 or older and the parent or guardian of any child added. We obtain parental consent through:

- the parent creating and controlling the account and expressly agreeing to the Terms of Service and this policy; and
- a **payment-card transaction** through our payment provider, which confirms the account holder is an adult. In the **United States** this is a **refundable US$1 verification charge** taken when the trial starts and then refunded; in other regions it is the subscription payment. A monetary card transaction — whether taken immediately or at an agreed future time — is a recognised method of verifying that the account holder is an adult.

**A parent's controls.** At any time a parent or guardian may:

**What we share about a child.** We do not make a child's personal information public except for the limited leaderboard display described in Section 4.2. ## 12. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights. These rights are not absolute and may be subject to legal exceptions.

- **Access / know** — request a copy of, and information about, the personal information we hold about you or your child.
- **Correct** — ask us to fix inaccurate or incomplete information.
- **Delete** — ask us to delete personal information we hold.
- **Portability** — receive certain information in a portable format.
- **Restrict / object** — ask us to limit or stop certain processing.
- **Withdraw consent** — where we rely on consent, withdraw it at any time (including a parent withdrawing consent for a child).
- **Opt out of sale/sharing/targeted advertising** — although note that we do **not** sell or share personal information or use it for targeted advertising.
- **Lodge a complaint** — with us and/or your local data-protection authority.
- **Appeal** — where available, appeal a decision we make about your request by replying to our response.

To exercise any right, email **privacy@thekidsgate.com** (or support@thekidsgate.com). We may need to verify your identity before responding, and a parent may exercise these rights on behalf of their child. You may use an authorised agent where the law allows, subject to proof of authorisation. We will respond within the time required by applicable law and will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

### Region-specific notes

- **Australia (Privacy Act 1988 & Australian Privacy Principles):** You may complain to us first; if unsatisfied, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.
- **United States (COPPA & state privacy laws such as California's CCPA/CPRA):** Parents have the specific rights in Section 5 over their children's information. California residents have rights of access, deletion, correction, and to opt out of sale/sharing — noting we do not sell or share.
- **Indonesia (Personal Data Protection Law / UU PDP No. 27 of 2022):** You have rights of access, correction, erasure, and withdrawal of consent, and may lodge complaints with the relevant authority.
- **Thailand (PDPA B.E. 2562):** You have rights of access, correction, erasure, restriction, objection, portability, and to withdraw consent, and may complain to the Personal Data Protection Committee.
- **EU/UK:** The Services are not offered to, or aimed at, users in the EU or UK, and we do not knowingly process their personal information. If you believe we hold your data, contact us and we will delete it.
